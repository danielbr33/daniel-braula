Engineering Studies – Warsaw University of Technology

I completed my engineering degree in Automation and Robotics at the Faculty of Power and Aeronautical Engineering, Warsaw University of Technology. During this time, I developed a strong foundation in both classical and modern engineering disciplines, combining theory with hands-on experience.

The curriculum included a broad range of fundamental subjects that every engineer should know — from mechanics and structural strength, through fluid dynamics, to robotic motion planning, degrees of freedom analysis, and industrial robot control.

I also became proficient with many tools and simulation environments used in industry and academia, including:

  • MATLAB – for modeling, simulation and control systems design
  • ANSYS and ADAMS – for mechanical simulations
  • FEM (Finite Element Method) – for structural analysis
  • NX and SolidWorks – for CAD design and prototyping

Alongside my studies, I actively participated in the Robotics Student Research Group (KNR), where I joined the electronics team for an autonomous car project, contributing to hardware and firmware development.

My engineering thesis involved designing and implementing a two-degree-of-freedom balancing platform using LabVIEW. The platform was equipped with a real-time control system designed to stabilize its motion and respond to disturbances.

These studies shaped my engineering mindset, taught me precision, analytical thinking, and gave me the tools to design and control complex electromechanical systems.
